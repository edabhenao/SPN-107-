Spanish for Heritage Speakers (Foundational Level)

A 14-Week Academic Literacy & Linguistics Curriculum for Community College Students

Course Overview: This course is tailored for students who grew up hearing or speaking Spanish at home but have had little to no formal academic training in the language. The curriculum focuses on expanding vocabulary, mastering spelling and written accents, distinguishing between formal and informal registers, and celebrating bilingual identity while eliminating academic insecurities.

Week 1: Identity, Language, & Belonging

Sociolinguistics
Topics & Subtopics:
  • Introduction to Heritage Linguistics: What is a "heritage speaker"? Combating linguistic insecurity (the "I don't speak good Spanish" myth).
  • Linguistic Variation: Honoring regional dialects and family vernaculars as valid forms of communication.
  • Diagnostic Assessment: Baseline evaluation of reading comprehension and intuitive writing.

Week 2: Mapping Sound to Print (The Alphabet & Phonetics)

Orthography
Topics & Subtopics:
  • The Spanish Sound System: Connecting the sounds students know implicitly to written letters.
  • Problematic Letter Pairs: Mastering B vs. V, and the silent H.
  • The Vowel System: Pure Spanish vowels vs. English diphthongs (avoiding English vowel sounds in writing).

Week 3: Syllables & The Invisible Stress

Phonetics & Accents
Topics & Subtopics:
  • Syllable Division (Silabeo): How to break words down into written syllables.
  • Strong and Weak Vowels: Understanding Diphthongs (diptongos) and Hiatus (hiatos).
  • The Tonic Syllable (Sílaba tónica): Locating where the voice naturally rises in a word without looking at written accents.

Week 4: Mastering Written Accents (Tildes) - Part 1

Grammar & Orthography
Topics & Subtopics:
  • The Classification of Words: Agudas, Graves (Llanas), and Esdrújulas.
  • The Rules of the Accent Mark: When to physically write a tilde based on the ending letter (N, S, or Vowel).
  • Application Practice: Adding accents to familiar spoken words.

Week 5: Written Accents Part 2 & The Written Word

Literacy Skills
Topics & Subtopics:
  • Diacritical Accents (Acento diacrítico): Distinguishing homonyms via accents (e.g., tú vs. tu, él vs. el, sí vs. si).
  • Introduction to Reading Strategies: Reading short contemporary essays/blogs by Latinx authors to analyze sentence structure.

Week 6: Navigating Language Contact (Spanglish & Borrowings)

Vocabulary & Culture
Topics & Subtopics:
  • Code-switching vs. Borrowing: The mechanics of Spanglish and its cultural value.
  • Calques and Loanwords: Identifying and adapting anglicisms (e.g., parquear, chatear, aseguranza).
  • Linguistic Contexts: When to use informal code-switching vs. when standard professional Spanish is expected.

Week 7: The "Tricky" Consonants & Midterm Review

Orthography & Assessment
Topics & Subtopics:
  • The Seseo and Phonetic Overlap: Mastering S, C, and Z for Spanish speakers from the Americas.
  • The Double Letters: Correct usage of G vs. J, and LL vs. Y.
  • Midterm Exam / Portfolio Showcase: Assessment of written accent mastery and phonetic spelling.

Week 8: Expanding Vocabulary & Formal Registers

Formal Writing
Topics & Subtopics:
  • False Cognates (Falsos amigos): Avoiding common pitfalls (e.g., embarazada does not mean embarrassed; introducir vs. presentar).
  • Synonym Expansion: Replacing overused words (like hacer, tener, cosa) with precise academic vocabulary.
  • Register Shift: Moving text from text-message shorthand to professional/academic phrasing.

Week 9: Structural Foundations (Nouns, Gender, & Agreements)

Grammar Mechanics
Topics & Subtopics:
  • Tricky Gender Rules: Nouns ending in -ma (el problema, el sistema) and -ción/-dad (la nación, la comunidad).
  • Noun-Adjective Agreement: Correcting mismatched pluralization and gender conventions in extended writing.
  • The Personal "A": Restoring the structural "a" when referring to people, which is often dropped in informal speech.

Week 10: Taming the Past Tense (Preterite vs. Imperfect)

Verb Mechanics
Topics & Subtopics:
  • The Two Past Tenses: Untangling Pretérito Indefinido (completed actions) vs. Pretérito Imperfecto (descriptions/habits) in written storytelling.
  • Orthographic Shifts in Verbs: Spelled changes in the past tense (e.g., buscar → busqué, llegar → llegué).
  • Writing Focus: Composing a narrative about a childhood or family memory.

Week 11: Introduction to the Subjunctive Mood

Advanced Grammar
Topics & Subtopics:
  • Demystifying the Subjunctive: Recognizing how students already use the subjunctive orally to express wishes, doubts, and recommendations.
  • Formal Spelling of the Present Subjunctive: Trigger phrases (e.g., Quiero que..., Es importante que...).
  • Common Errors: Eradicating non-standard spoken structures (e.g., correcting "haiga" to "haya").

Week 12: Professional Communication & Digital Literacy

Practical Application
Topics & Subtopics:
  • Formal Email Etiquette: Openings, professional closings (Atentamente, Cordialmente), and proper use of Usted.
  • Accents on Digital Keyboards: How to configure and use Spanish accent marks and the ñ on smartphones and computers.
  • Translation Basics: The ethics and challenges of acting as informal family interpreters.

Week 13: Introduction to Textual Analysis & Essay Structure

Composition
Topics & Subtopics:
  • Transition Words (Conectores lógicos): Using words like sin embargo, por lo tanto, además, en resumen to link complex ideas.
  • Structuring an Argument: Writing a clear thesis sentence and organizing short introductory, body, and concluding paragraphs.
  • Peer Review Workshop: Collaboratively editing spelling and accent marks in a short opinion essay.

Week 14: Final Presentations & Linguistic Pride

Conclusion
Topics & Subtopics:
  • Final Oral Presentation: Students present an investigation into their family's country of origin, community history, or a bilingual cultural phenomenon.
  • Course Reflections: Embracing fully bilingual literacy as a superpower in academic and corporate spaces.
  • Final Exam/Portfolio Submission.
